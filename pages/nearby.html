Sarnath

Sarnath, located near Varanasi, is a sacred Buddhist site where Gautama Buddha delivered his first sermon after attaining enlightenment. This historic event marked the beginning of Buddhist teachings and the establishment of the Sangha. Significant attractions include the Dhamek Stupa, Ashoka Pillar, and the Sarnath Museum housing the Lion Capital of Ashoka, according to Varanasi Smart City.

Mirzapur

Mirzapur, in Uttar Pradesh, is known for its carpet and brassware industries. This historically significant city, located along the Ganges River, boasts a rich heritage and natural beauty. It's a pilgrimage site with the famous Vindhyavasini Devi temple at Vindhyachal Dham. Additionally, it features scenic waterfalls and the historic Chunar Fort

Prayagraj

Prayagraj, located in Uttar Pradesh, is a sacred city famed for the Triveni Sangam, the confluence of the Ganga, Yamuna, and mythical Saraswati rivers. It hosts the Maha Kumbh Mela, the world's largest religious gathering, every twelve years. The city blends spirituality, history (e.g., Allahabad Fort), and culture. It's a significant pilgrimage destination and former political hub

Kanpur

Kanpur, Uttar Pradesh's industrial capital, sits on the Ganges River. It is known for its leather and textile industries. The city is also rich in history and culture. Key attractions include the Kanpur Zoological Park and Nana Rao Park, reflecting both British colonial influence and local heritage like the Kanpur Memorial Church.
